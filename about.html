About Us

In the heart of India's vibrant landscape lies Uttar Pradesh, a state where every city has a story, every village has a legend, and every monument has a saga. Uttar Pradesh Tourism is your gateway to exploring this land of diverse cultures, ancient history, and spiritual enlightenment. Our journey through Uttar Pradesh begins with its rich tapestry of history. The state has been the backdrop for significant events that have shaped India's destiny. It was here that Lord Rama was born in Ayodhya, setting the stage for the epic Ramayana. In Mathura, the air still resonates with tales of Lord Krishna's childhood, making it a pivotal site for Hindu pilgrims. As we delve deeper into the state's historical narrative, we encounter the Mughal influence in Agra. The city is home to the Taj Mahal, an ivory-white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is not just a monument but a testament to love that transcends time. Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri further narrate the glory of Mughal architecture and ingenuity. The intricate carvings and robust fortifications speak volumes about the grandeur of the era gone by. Moving to Lucknow, the 'City of Nawabs,' one is greeted with an entirely different yet equally enchanting cultural experience. The city's refined manners, exquisite cuisine, ornate architecture, and the lilting melody of classical music reflect its rich Awadhi heritage. The spiritual essence of Uttar Pradesh is best experienced in Varanasi. Also known as Kashi or Benaras, this city on the banks of the Ganges is considered one of the holiest places in India. The evening Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a divine spectacle that draws souls seeking solace and salvation. Allahabad (Prayagraj), where the sacred rivers Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati confluence, hosts the Kumbh Mela, one of the largest religious gatherings in the world. The city's Triveni Sangam is a focal point for spiritual cleansing and ritual baths. The state's cultural vibrancy is showcased through its festivals. Holi in Barsana and Mathura is a colorful extravaganza, while Diwali in Ayodhya is celebrated with thousands of earthen lamps illuminating the night sky. Uttar Pradesh's cuisine is as diverse as its geography. From the kebabs and biryanis of Lucknow to the sweet Petha of Agra and Banarasi Paan, every dish has a flavor that lingers long after the meal is over. For nature enthusiasts, Uttar Pradesh offers sanctuaries like Dudhwa National Park and Chambal Wildlife Sanctuary, where one can witness India's rich flora and fauna in their natural habitat.
